Villa Colle Ulivi · The Pool
04
Shared space
The Panoramic Pool

The panoramic pool of Villa Colle Ulivi is one of its most treasured features. Surrounded by ancient olive trees and sun loungers, it offers a breathtaking view over the gentle Tuscan hills. Available seasonally from May to September (weather permitting), it is shared by guests of both apartments.

Hours and rules
  • Hours: 9:00 – 20:00 · Season: May – September
  • Depth: 1.40 metres — no jumping or diving from the edge
  • Shower and foot disinfection required before entering
  • Wait at least 3 hours after eating before swimming
  • Children under 12 must be supervised by an adult at all times
  • Footwear required in the pool area (slippery surface)
  • No glass bottles or glasses allowed
  • Upon leaving: close umbrellas and the wooden gate
  • No lifeguard on duty — use at your own risk
What to expect
  • Panoramic views over the Tuscan hills
  • Ancient olive trees all around
  • Sun loungers and umbrellas
  • Access from both apartments
  • Quiet and private atmosphere
  • Crystal-clear water, regularly maintained
Important

The pool is shared with guests of the other apartment. We invite you to respect the common spaces and enjoy this corner of paradise with care and consideration for those who share it with you. The villa is a historic property — please treat it as your own home.

Villa Colle Ulivi · Hiking & Nature
08
Walking through the hills
Hiking & Trails

The countryside around Villa Colle Ulivi is a paradise for nature walkers. Trails wind through olive groves, chestnut forests, medieval villages and extraordinary panoramic viewpoints. Most routes are suitable for dogs on a leash — the area is pet friendly.

✦ Trail from the Villa — Not to be missed
The Fairy Tale Path
Villa Colle Ulivi → Collodi · Pinocchio Park
Easy ~8 km · ~2h 30min 🐾 Pet Friendly

The trail starts just 2 minutes walk from the villa and winds through olive groves, woods and bucolic Tuscan landscapes all the way to Collodi — the medieval village where Carlo Lorenzini set the adventures of Pinocchio. The path is well-marked, shaded and suitable for everyone, including children and dogs on a leash. The return can be made on foot or by taxi/car. An early morning start is recommended to enjoy the cool air and golden light over the olive trees.

Ancient Olive Grove Trail
Easy 2–3 km 🐾 Pet Friendly

A loop through the ancient olive trees surrounding the villa. Starting from the property gate, with views over the hills and villages. Perfect for morning or sunset, suitable for all levels including young children. Duration approx. 45 minutes.

Uzzano — The Castle Among the Hills
Moderate 6 km 🐾 Pet Friendly

A trail climbing towards the medieval village of Uzzano, with its stone alleyways and panoramic tower. The route passes through oak forests and vineyards with views over the Valdinievole. Great for photography. Moderate elevation gain.

Buggiano Castello — The Hidden Village
Moderate 5 km 🐾 Pet Friendly

A route through olive groves and fields towards the medieval village of Buggiano Castello, one of the best-preserved in the area, with its Romanesque church and stone houses. Dirt track, suitable with comfortable walking shoes.

Montalbano Trail
Challenging 12–15 km 🐾 Pet Friendly

A longer and more demanding route through the Montalbano massif, among chestnut forests, olive groves and breathtaking panoramas over the Valdinievole, Florence and the coast. Ideal for experienced hikers. Bring plenty of water and proper hiking boots.

🐾
All trails are pet friendly

Dogs are welcome on all listed trails. Remember the leash, water for your four-legged friend and poo bags. The Tuscan countryside is perfect for exploring together.

Villa Colle Ulivi · Hidden Villages
09
Treasures to discover
The Hidden Villages

Far from the tourist crowds, the countryside around Pescia hides little-known medieval masterpieces. Stone villages, Romanesque churches, ancient towers and timeless panoramas. These are our favourites to explore.

15 min
15 minutes · ★ Must-see
Montecatini Alto
The medieval hilltop village above Montecatini Terme, reachable by a historic funicular. Silent alleyways, a square with views over the plain, the Romanesque church of San Pietro and a timeless atmosphere. No cars, just peace.
20 min
20 minutes · Vino DOC
Montecarlo di Lucca
A medieval gem on the Lucca hills, famous for its Montecarlo DOC — one of Tuscany's most elegant white wines. Intact medieval walls, a 14th-century fortress and cellars where you can taste the local wines.
10 min
10 minutes · Medievale
Buggiano Castello
One of the best-preserved medieval villages in the Valdinievole. Cobbled streets, stone houses, the oratory of Santa Maria della Neve and a panoramic tower. Very few tourists — authentic and genuine atmosphere.
15 min
15 minutes · Panoramic
Uzzano
A small hilltop village with a medieval tower and extraordinary views over the Valdinievole. Walking up through olive groves and vineyards you reach the historic centre, with the church of San Jacopo and walls that tell centuries of history.
25 min
25 minutes · IGP Lenticchie
Sorana
A small village on the Pescia hills, famous for the prized Sorana Lentils IGP — a gastronomic rarity to be savoured on the spot. Unspoilt landscape, fresh air and a local market where you can buy authentic products.
10 min
10 minutes · Romanico
Pietrabuona
A medieval village in pietra serena with the beautiful Romanesque parish church of San Tommaso. Small but perfectly preserved, with postcard-worthy views of the surrounding hills. Often overlooked by tourists — all yours.
Host's tip

Visiting these villages in the morning or late afternoon gives unforgettable light and atmosphere. Avoid the midday hours in summer. Wear comfortable shoes — the cobbled streets can be uneven.

Almost all these villages have a small bar or osteria for a break. Always try the house wine and ask about local products. Residents are generally happy to share the history of their village.
